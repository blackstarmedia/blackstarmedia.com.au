★ Prompt Playbook

20 Suno tips to better your prompts.

Straight from the AI:M Workshops desk — a field guide to writing prompts that turn Suno from a slot machine into an instrument. Clearer prompts make better songs.

Clearer prompts Better songs More control More you
01

Be Clear & Specific

  1. Be clear about the mood and energy level.
  2. Use a specific genre, not just “good music”.
02

Mood & Emotion

  1. Use emotion words like “haunting” or “euphoric”.
  2. Describe the emotional arc — let the feeling lead.
03

Genre & Style

  1. Specify the primary genre, then a subgenre.
  2. Try genre + era, e.g. “90s grunge”.
04

Instrumentation

  1. List your key instruments.
  2. Use “minimal” or “lush” to set the layering.
05

Vocals & Lyrics

  1. Specify vocal style — male, female, or none.
  2. Tell a story or set a scene with the lyrics.
06

Structure & Form

  1. Specify structure: intro, verse, chorus, bridge.
  2. Build slowly or start strong?
07

Production & Sound

  1. Lo-fi or hi-fi? Clean, gritty, or warm?
  2. Add reverb, delay or distortion for texture.
08

Reference & Inspiration

  1. Name artists you like, e.g. “like Billie Eilish”.
  2. Reference songs, albums or eras.
09

Advanced Prompting

  1. Use commas to separate ideas; front-load what matters.
  2. Use brackets for emphasis, e.g. (Epic chorus).
10

Experiment & Refine

  1. Change one thing at a time and test variations.
  2. Save your best prompts — have fun with it.
